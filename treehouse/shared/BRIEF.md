# Tree House Boutique Hotel — Design Brief (Shared DNA)

This brief is **shared across all 3 Treehouse variants**. Every builder agent must read this before starting.

---

## The Brand

**Tree House Boutique Hotel** — adults-only luxury boutique hotel in the historic Santa Ana neighborhood of Mérida, Yucatán, México. 15 rooms. First **Michelin Key** hotel in Mérida. Colonial charm meets contemporary comfort. Lush greenery creates a naturally cool microclimate inside the property — the "tree house" name is *literal* (the building is wrapped in foliage and gardens).

**Address:** Calle 43 × 58 y 60, #489, Santa Ana, Mérida, México 97000

**Brand personality:** insider, curated, botanical, serene. Adults-only — so this is a *grown-up retreat*, not family-friendly. The Michelin Key gives it real prestige; lean on it.

**Reference for what already exists:** https://treehouseboutiquehotel.com/

---

## Distinguishing from Boutique by The Museo

Tree House and Boutique by The Museo are **sister properties** (same group). Both are 15-room luxury hotels in Mérida. So the designs must feel *related but distinct*. Key differentiators for Tree House:

- **Adults-only** (Boutique is not exclusively adults-only)
- **Michelin Key certified** (a real accolade — feature it)
- **Botanical / "tree house" identity** — lean into greens, vines, foliage, courtyards-as-gardens, the literal arboreal feel of the property
- **Santa Ana neighborhood** (vs. Boutique's Paseo de Montejo / Palacio Cantón location)
- **Has a Journal** — curated travel guides; can be a section
- **"5+ nights = 30% off" offer** (vs. Boutique's "6+ nights = airport transfer + gift")

The same Treehouse × SoHo Galleries art collaboration runs here (this is the property where "Treehouse" comes from — fitting that Tree House Hotel is the namesake/anchor location).

---

## Story Pillars

1. **The Botanical Sanctuary** — adults-only, 15 rooms, gardens that cool the air, Santa Ana's quiet
2. **The Michelin Key** — first in Mérida, what that means, what it signals
3. **The Curated Mérida** — Journal entries, "hidden spaces, seasonal secrets", insider guides
4. **The Treehouse × SoHo Galleries Art Collab** — Tree House is the anchor venue; the collaboration *lives* here

---

## Style Inspirations (re-anchored for Tree House — same trio, retuned for the botanical/Michelin character)

- **V1 Cinematic Immersive** → https://thelargo.com/ (Porto). Same anchor as Boutique V1, but with **botanical** dressing — green tones in the gradient overlays, the hero might feature a foliage-wrapped courtyard rather than colonial limestone.
- **V2 Editorial Magazine** → https://masgirbau.com/ (Catalonia). Same anchor as Boutique V2, but with a **field-journal** feel — closer to Mas Girbau's "natural" emphasis. Botanical illustrations or hairline botanical motifs feel right here.
- **V3 Brutalist Art-Forward** → Peter & Paul Hotel + Pan & Koffee V3 grid. Same anchor as Boutique V3 — but here the gallery/art-catalog framing is even MORE earned because Tree House is the actual venue for Treehouse × SoHo Galleries.

## Color Palette (Tree House — *botanical / greener than Boutique*)

```
--moss:          #2f4a32   /* deep moss green — primary brand */
--canopy:        #1a2e1f   /* near-black green for backgrounds */
--leaf:          #6b8e5f   /* mid-green, lighter */
--sage:          #b5c4a8   /* dusty green pale */
--ivory:         #f7f3eb   /* warm ivory paper */
--limestone:     #ecdfc9   /* warm wash */
--ochre:         #c69960   /* warm gold accent */
--terracotta:    #b85a3a   /* used sparingly — Michelin red echo */
--ink:           #14110d   /* near-black for headlines */
--bark:          #5b4632   /* warm dark brown for body text */
```

Per variant:
- V1: dark — `--canopy` background, ivory text, moss + ochre accents
- V2: light — `--ivory` background, bark/ink text, sage + ochre accents (with botanical hairlines)
- V3: high-contrast — limestone + canopy alternating sections, terracotta + moss accents, mono in moss

---

## Typography

- **Display serifs:** DM Serif Display, Cormorant, Playfair Display
- **Body serifs:** Cormorant Garamond, EB Garamond
- **Sans:** Inter
- **Mono (V3):** JetBrains Mono, IBM Plex Mono
- **Optional hand-feel:** for botanical hairline labels, consider a thin italic serif like Cormorant 300 italic

V1: refined serif + Inter. V2: serif-heavy + small-caps sans. V3: mono + condensed sans.

## Required Sections (every variant)

1. **Hero** — name, tagline, Michelin Key call-out, primary CTA (Reserve / Book)
2. **The Sanctuary** — adults-only, 15 rooms, botanical microclimate, Santa Ana
3. **The Michelin Key** — feature this prestige (small dedicated moment)
4. **Rooms** — 15 rooms, king/queen options (no specific named room types from source — invent in-character names or describe as "King Garden Room", "Queen Courtyard Suite", "Penthouse Canopy", etc., 4 categories)
5. **The Treehouse × SoHo Galleries Art Collab** — centerpiece (Tree House is the namesake venue)
6. **The Journal** — short list of curated guides ("Hidden cenotes of Yucatán", "A morning at the market", etc.)
7. **Curated Mérida** — experiences/services
8. **Voices / Reviews** — testimonial section
9. **Location** — Santa Ana, Calle 43
10. **Offers + Footer** — 5+ nights = 30% off

## Copy Voice

Match the inspiration anchor + Tree House's brand:
- V1: cinematic + botanical — "Where the courtyard breathes. Fifteen rooms. One canopy."
- V2: editorial + field-journal — drop caps, longer prose, Spanish phrases, plant names threaded as labels
- V3: art-catalog + gallery — taxonomic, the building as specimen, Michelin Key as data-point

Bilingual: lean *more* into Spanish than Boutique because the Santa Ana barrio feels more local than the Paseo de Montejo gallery district.

---

## Technical Requirements

Same as Boutique brief. Self-contained HTML/CSS/JS, no frameworks. Mobile responsive (768px, 480px). Accessibility. Performance.

---

## What "amazing" means here

- The greenery / botanical identity must come through *immediately* — not just one image; treat the green palette as a character
- The Michelin Key must feel earned, not pasted-on
- The art collab section must feel like Tree House is the natural home for it (because it is)
- Echo of the Boutique by The Museo design language, but distinct enough that a returning visitor sees them as siblings, not duplicates
