## New voice trajectory

The page now reads as one writer with one set of habits:

- **Specifics over abstractions** — 1898, 22°C, three steps up, two flamboyanes, brass tap, ten o'clock cochinita
- **Operational asides** — "the one we put friends in", "our driver knows which gate to use", "she keeps asking us to recommend"
- **The wry adults register** — "no reason to be downstairs by nine", "the cathedral two blocks away you'll keep walking past", "anyone who has finished apologizing for sleeping in"
- **Restraint on adjectives** — the prose leans on nouns and verbs; almost no "elegant / curated / refined / exquisite" survives
- **Spanish that does work** — the interstitial, the mercado phrase, *Mérida a tu medida* — all load-bearing, none decorative

## Distinct from sibling Boutique V1

Cross-checked Boutique V1's copy: their material trio is **Cal / Madera / Patio** (limestone / mahogany / patio). Tree House's trio remains **Canopy / Adults Only / Barrio** — orthogonal vocabulary. Boutique's testimonials use first-person family scenes ("My husband does not nap. He napped here."). Tree House's now use a different register: hedged, complaining-but-loyal, brief. Boutique's pull quote: *"Un refugio en el corazón de cal."* — Tree House's now: *"No hay recepción. Hay alguien que ya sabe en qué silla cae la última luz."* — different image, different rhythm. Boutique CTAs say "Reserve"; Tree House CTAs say "Hold / Walk / Write / Find." The two sites should now read as the work of two writers visiting the same city.

## For R3 (motion / polish)

These were out of scope for the copy pass but worth noting:

1. The interstitial section now has more Spanish text than before (a full sentence). Consider type sizing: the English gloss should sit smaller / lighter than the Spanish, since the Spanish is the load-bearing line. Currently both share the same treatment from R1.
2. Sanctuary now has three body paragraphs where before it had two. Verify vertical rhythm — the section may want slightly more air between paragraphs, or a soft rule between p2 and p3 (the adults-only paragraph) to mark the tonal shift.
3. The Michelin cells are longer now (concrete prose vs criteria-recitation). Verify the grid doesn't bloat; if it does, consider letting cell 03 sit visually slighter than 01 & 02, since 03 is now the shortest by content.
4. Testimonial timing: the one-line "Best hotel breakfast in México. That's the whole review." benefits from a slightly faster auto-advance cadence than the longer testimonials — worth a motion tweak so the page rhythm matches the content rhythm.
5. The Spanish phrase inside the Mercado journal excerpt (*se acaba cuando se acaba*) is currently in italic via `<em>` — verify it doesn't conflict with the italic-noun discipline established in R1. Italic for non-English language is conventional and should read as language-marking rather than emphasis, but a designer's eye should confirm in context.
6. The CTA "Write to the house →" replaces a button labeled "Enquire". The button width will change; verify the reserve form layout doesn't break on narrower viewports.

## One finding documented and partially declined

**Finding #5 (full Spanish translation of the no-front-desk paragraph side-by-side)** — the critic's strongest version of this fix asks for a paired Spanish/English presentation or an alternating-by-viewport treatment. Both require structural change (additional element or viewport-conditional logic) that the brief explicitly rules out ("Do not change section structure, IDs, class names"). The compromise: the no-front-desk line now appears in Spanish at the interstitial (a section that already exists), echoes in Pillar III's rhythm, and is the lead body paragraph in English in Sanctuary. The reader who reads in Spanish gets a load-bearing Spanish version of the page's best line; the reader who reads in English gets the line at full weight. If R3 opens the door to structural changes, a paired presentation in Sanctuary should be the first move.
