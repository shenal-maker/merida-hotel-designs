N° 01 · La Casa Verde
El Refugio Botánico

20°58'N · 89°37'W — Barrio de Santa Ana, Mérida

A house wrapped in leaves, kept quiet on purpose.

There is no front desk. There is a person who already knows your flight, your coffee, and which courtyard chair gets the last of the sun.

A colonial house in Santa Ana, smothered in the kind of greenery that cools the air ten degrees before you've finished breathing in. Built in 1898, quietly opened after a four-year restoration in 2019. The trees that wrap it are older — one mature higuera through the courtyard, two flamboyanes leaning over the south wall, and fifty years of botanical accumulation that nobody had the heart to thin.

Adults only — for couples on their second decade, friends on their fortieth year, and anyone who has finished apologizing for sleeping in. The pool is open at five in the morning. The bar pours until the last guest. The mornings, again, start when you do.

I Canopy 32°C on Calle 43 at three in the afternoon. 22°C under the leaves at the same hour. The difference is the house.
II Adults Only Fifteen keys. No children. No lobby music. No reason to be downstairs by nine.
III Barrio Santa Ana — the plaza that empties earliest, the cathedral two blocks away you'll keep walking past.
La Llave Michelin

The first Michelin Key in Mérida.

Michelin began handing out Keys in 2025 — finally rating the rooms the way they've rated the kitchens for a century. Of the hotels in Mérida, they named ours.

01 What the inspectors noticed. The cook brings coffee out before you ask. The bath fills from a brass tap that takes its time. The leaves above the courtyard are not pruned. We were told afterward that these were the things — the lack of hurry.
02 What we didn't change for them. The fountain still gurgles unevenly. The middle stair still creaks at the third step. Breakfast is still whenever you ask. The house was doing what it does the day they came, and the day after.
03 First in Mérida. One in the city. The 2025 guide named exactly one property in the Yucatán capital. This one.

An Ongoing Collaboration · Anchor Venue

TreehousexSoHo Galleries

The collaboration is named for this house. The collection sleeps where you do.

Treehouse is the name of a gallery program. It is also the name of this house, because this house is where the program started. Drawings in the corridors. Ceramics on the courtyard ledge. Photography at the foot of the stairs. The collection rotates four times a year — and the work sleeps where you sleep. The rooms are not its backdrop. They are the room.
