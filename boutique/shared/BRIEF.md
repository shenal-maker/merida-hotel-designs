# Boutique by The Museo — Design Brief (Shared DNA)

This brief is **shared across all 3 variants**. Every builder agent must read this before starting.

---

## The Brand

**Boutique by The Museo** is an intimate 15-room luxury hotel in the historic center of Mérida, Yucatán, México. Located steps from Palacio Cantón on Paseo de Montejo. Restored hacienda architecture, uncompromising privacy, a quiet refuge for the modern traveler.

**Brand personality:** refined, culturally aware, hospitality-with-soul. Not a resort. Not corporate. Whisper, don't shout.

**Reference for *what already exists*:** https://boutiquebythemuseo.com/

---

## The Story Pillars (use all three across every variant)

1. **The Sanctuary** — 15 rooms only. Privacy. Restoration. The hush of a colonial courtyard.
2. **The Curated Mérida** — Honeymoon packages, cenotes & haciendas expeditions, in-suite wellness, private airport transfers.
3. **The Treehouse × SoHo Galleries Art Collaboration** *(NEW — required section in every variant)*
   - Treehouse is the gallery space concept. SoHo Galleries (Mérida) is the partner.
   - Curated art rotates through the property. Guests live inside the exhibition.
   - This section must feel *substantive*, not bolted on — it is the differentiator.

---

## Style Inspirations (one per variant — your *anchor*)

- **V1 Cinematic Immersive** → https://thelargo.com/ (Porto). Carousel-driven. Warm-tone photography. Sophisticated literary copy. Black/white neutrals + ochre warmth from imagery. Refined minimalism with cultural depth.
- **V2 Editorial Magazine** → https://masgirbau.com/ (Catalonia). Earthy palette (cream / stone / forest green / ochre). Poetic Spanish-language copy. Three-element thematic rhythm (Mas Girbau does "Piedra / Hogar / Madera"). Heritage-focused, contemplative. *"Peace, history, and silence."*
- **V3 Brutalist Art-Forward** → https://www.peterandpaulnola.com/ (New Orleans — historic convent-turned-hotel; eclectic, vintage-meets-modern, art-heavy, slightly irreverent). Combine that art-gallery sensibility with the Pan & Koffee V3 grid/typography brutalism — make the art collab the centerpiece.

## Color Palette (Boutique-adapted — *use these, not the Pan & Koffee coffee tones*)

```
--ochre:        #c69960   /* warm gold-brown */
--cenote:       #1f5e64   /* deep teal-green (cenote water + tropical) */
--earth:        #5b4632   /* warm dark brown for text */
--ink:          #1a1410   /* near-black for headlines */
--stone:        #8a7e6b   /* mid-tone neutral */

```

- V1: dark mode, ink + ochre + cream cinematic palette
- V2: light mode, ivory + sage + ochre editorial palette
- V3: high-contrast, limestone + ink + terracotta brutalist palette

## Typography

Pick from these — match the variant:

- **Display serifs:** DM Serif Display, Cormorant, Playfair Display
- **Body serifs:** Cormorant Garamond, EB Garamond
- **Sans:** Inter, Söhne fallback `system-ui`
- **Mono (V3):** JetBrains Mono, IBM Plex Mono

V1 leans into a refined serif + Inter combo. V2 is editorial — serif-heavy with a single elegant sans. V3 is mono + a strong condensed sans.

7. **Reviews / Voices** — pull a few testimonial-style quotes (you can invent in-character ones if no source data)
8. **Booking / CTA / Footer** — Resident's Allocation offer (6+ nights = airport transfer + gift), Summer Special (20% off Jun 1 – Aug 31, 2026)

## Copy Voice

Match the inspiration anchor:
- V1: evocative, cinematic, single-sentence rhythms ("A courtyard. Fifteen rooms. The hush before sunrise.")
- V2: editorial-magazine, drop-caps, longer prose, Spanish phrases threaded ("Un refugio. A sanctuary in the limestone heart of Mérida.")
- V3: art-catalog / data-driven, ALL CAPS for tags, mono for metadata, art-installation copy treatment

Use Spanish/English bilingual phrasing naturally (Mérida is in México). Examples: "Desde Mérida", "Un refugio", "Calle 43", "Paseo de Montejo".

## Technical Requirements

- Single `index.html` per variant + `css/style.css` + `js/main.js`
- Self-contained — no build step, no frameworks
- Mobile responsive (test breakpoints at 768px, 480px)
- Fonts via Google Fonts CDN
- Accessibility: skip link, semantic HTML, alt text, aria-labels on nav
- Performance: lazy-load images below fold, preload hero
- Reuse the structural patterns from the Pan & Koffee variants — those already work well

## What "amazing" means here

- The first 800ms must feel deliberate (hero, fonts loaded, no FOUT chaos)
- The art collab section must feel like it could be a gallery's own site
- Every section earns its place — no filler
- The hotel's quietness must come through; this is NOT a flashy "look at me" site
