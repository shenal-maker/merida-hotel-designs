### MAJOR

**#3 — Critic pull-quote slipping into hotel-voice.** Rewrote second sentence to
a *specific* critical observation the hotel itself wouldn't write:

> "The Manzón bronze in the library alcove is doing the work three signs and a
> velvet rope would otherwise do; you lower your voice without being asked."

Citation upgraded from "No. 18" to *Cuaderno XVIII, otoño MMXXV* — gives the
attribution the weight of a real one.

**#4 — El Estándar's three identical pillar registers.** Differentiated:

- **Privacidad** → tightened to short staccato sentences with a sly close:
  *"…the staff know how you take your coffee — and by the third, they have stopped
  asking."*
- **Patrimonio** → lengthened into a tiny history (henequen family, cedar lintel,
  three-year restoration, the artisans who keep Centro Histórico standing).
- **Curaduría** → reduced to a single declarative sentence with a point of view:
  *"…we mean it the way a museum means it…"*

Three pillars now behave like three different pillars.

**#5 — Sanctuary lacks thesis.** Promoted the "what we kept / what we added"
diptych to the section's spine and rewrote the lede to set it up:

> *Un refugio.* A sanctuary in the limestone heart of Mérida — and, we discovered
> three months in, mostly an exercise in what to leave alone.

The drop-cap paragraph now ends on *"the list of what we had removed was longer
than the list of what we had put in"* — a thesis the rest of the section confirms.

**#6 — Testimonials straining credibility.** Three repairs + one addition:

- **E. Vargas** — rewritten completely. The line no longer pre-empts the brand's
  own objection; it now references the Pacheco henequen study in Plate XII (apparatus

- **NEW: R. Manríquez, Oaxaca** — added a Mexican voice quoted in Spanish only,
