N° 01 · El corredor al mediodía
La casa, despacio

20°58'N · 89°37'W — Centro Histórico, Mérida

A quiet kind of luxury, kept small on purpose.

Fifteen rooms. One courtyard. A restored colonial estate hidden one block from Paseo de Montejo. We did not build a hotel — we restored a house and then learned how to keep it whispering. The limestone holds yesterday's cool until afternoon, the mahogany doors swell in August and shrink by December, and the first light of day arrives through the courtyard before it finds the rooms.

There is no concierge desk. There is a person who noticed which way you walked yesterday, who has an opinion about whether the cenote is worth driving to today, and who will tell you the truth about both.

I Cal The walls hold yesterday's cool until afternoon. Fifteen keys, no lobby traffic, and a courtyard that belongs to whoever is in it.
II Madera Mahogany doors that need both hands. Ceilings raised back to their 1909 line, and tilework laid where the original tiles asked to stay.
III Patio A door between Palacio Cantón and the rest of the city. Most days, it stays closed.

"Some houses ask to be admired. Others ask to be lived in slowly. This one is the second kind."

— T. Mendoza, Treehouse · Field notes, January 2026

An Ongoing Collaboration · Estd. 2025

Treehouse × SoHo Galleries

A rotating exhibition lives inside the hotel. You are not visiting it. You are sleeping in it.

Treehouse curates for places that aren't museums. A hotel, a restaurant, a private courtyard — the rooms have a job, and the art has to share them. We installed a rotating collection at Boutique with SoHo Galleries in Mérida: drawings, ceramics, photography, the occasional uncomfortable thing, turning over four times a year. Guests live inside the exhibition rather than walking through it. Some pieces are for sale. Most are not.

None of them are for the lobby.

Calle 43, esquina con el Paseo

A block from Palacio Cantón.

Boutique by The Museo sits at the head of Paseo de Montejo — the city's grand avenue, planted with laurels in the 1900s and never finished hurrying. The Museum of Anthropology is one block away, the cathedral is a slow walk, and the airport is fifteen minutes by car.
