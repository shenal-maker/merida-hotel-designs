# How to film the hotel for the walk-in experience

A simple guide for whoever is holding the camera. No technical knowledge needed.

---

## What you need

- **A recent phone.** iPhone 14 or newer, or a Pixel 8 or newer. That's it.
- **A gimbal.** This is a small handheld stick that holds the phone steady while you walk. About $100. It makes a huge difference — without it the video shakes and the result looks blurry. Recommended models: *Insta360 Flow* or *DJI Osmo Mobile*. If you can't get one, walk very, very slowly.
- **The Polycam app.** Free to download. Sign up for the Pro plan ($8/month) — you can cancel after the shoot.

That's all. No extra cameras, no tripod, no fancy lighting.

---

## When to film

Pick a morning when:

- It's **calm** — no wind. Wind moves leaves, and moving leaves come out blurry in the final result.
- It's **slightly cloudy** OR you're filming right after sunrise. Bright sunshine creates dark shadows that confuse the technology.
- The hotel is **quiet** — fewer guests walking around. Tuesday or Wednesday mornings are usually best.

Total time on site: about **45 minutes to an hour** to film everything.

---

## Before you start filming

Open the Polycam app. Tap "Capture" → "Gaussian Splat."

The app will guide you through the settings. The key thing: **don't change settings during a shoot.** Once you start, hold the phone steady and walk.

---

## How to film one space

This is the most important section. Read it twice.

**Step 1.** Stand at the entrance of the space.

**Step 2.** Hold the phone at chest height, pointed slightly down so you can see the floor and walls.

**Step 3.** Start recording.

**Step 4.** Walk slowly through the space. Slower than you ever walk in real life — about one step per second.

**Step 5.** As you walk, slowly pan the phone around. Look at:
- The walls (slowly side to side)
- The ceiling (tilt up briefly)
- The floor (tilt down briefly)
- Corners and edges (these are important — don't skip them)
- Any interesting details (the ficus tree, a painting on the wall, the bed, the sink)

**Step 6.** When you reach the other side of the space, **stop, turn around, and walk back through it again**. This second pass should be at a different height — bend your knees slightly so the phone is lower, or stretch up so it's higher. The two heights together give the technology depth information.

**Step 7.** Stop recording.

**Time per space:** 90 seconds to 3 minutes. Shorter and the result has holes. Longer and you're wasting effort.

---

## The 5 spaces to capture, in order

Do them in this order in one continuous session. Don't film one space, then come back tomorrow for another — the lighting won't match.

1. **The courtyard with the ficus tree.** Start here, while the light is best. This is the most important one.
2. **The entry / lobby.** The front door, the logo on the wall, the first few steps inside.
3. **The pool and hammock area.** A relaxed moment.
4. **The King Garden Room.** Make the bed, turn on the lights, open the curtains. Stage it like you're expecting a guest in 10 minutes.
5. **The Penthouse Canopy.** Same — staged like a real guest is arriving.

**Optional 6:** the rooftop or terrace — save this for golden hour (about 30 minutes before sunset).

Between each space, walk to the next one — you don't need to stop the app, but pause the recording.

---

## Check the result before you leave

After each space:

1. In the Polycam app, **upload it to the cloud.** Takes 10–30 minutes to process.
2. Open the result in the app.
3. Look for:
   - **Holes** — places where the surface looks like nothing's there. Means your camera didn't see that area. Re-film that part of the space.
   - **Ghost leaves** — if the ficus tree's leaves look like fuzzy clouds instead of real leaves, the wind was too strong. Try filming again earlier in the morning when it's calmer.
   - **Blurry details** — if a wall or piece of furniture looks smeared, you walked past it too fast.

**If something fails, re-shoot before you leave the property.** You can't fix these problems later.

---

## Mistakes to avoid

- Walking too fast → blurry result
- Moving the phone in quick jerks → confusing for the technology
- Standing in one spot and just turning around → not enough movement
- Filming when people or pets are walking through → ghostly figures in the result
- Filming on a windy day → blurry leaves and plants
- Filming when sunlight is moving across walls (mid-morning, mid-afternoon) → lighting that drifts

---

## What happens after the shoot

1. Polycam processes each space (10–30 minutes per space).
2. Adele cleans up the results and embeds them on the website.
3. The walk-in experience goes live on the Tree House website.

Total turnaround from filming to live: about **a week**.

## Quick checklist for the day of filming

- [ ] Phone fully charged
- [ ] Gimbal charged
- [ ] Polycam app installed and logged in
- [ ] No wind / slight clouds
- [ ] Hotel is quiet
- [ ] 45 minutes blocked off
- [ ] Rooms staged (beds made, lights on, curtains open)
- [ ] Walk slowly
- [ ] Upload + check after each space
